What is a financial health score?

A financial health score rates your overall financial wellness across three dimensions: spending behavior, savings habits, and debt management. Unlike a credit score (which measures borrowing history), a financial health score measures how well you manage your day-to-day money.

What's a good financial health score?

Above 75 is excellent — you're spending wisely, saving consistently, and managing debt well. 50-75 means room for improvement in at least one area. Below 50 signals urgent changes needed. Most young Indians score 40-55 due to high spending and low emergency savings.
